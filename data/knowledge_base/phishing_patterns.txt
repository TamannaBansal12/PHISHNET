Phishing emails commonly use urgency, fear, or authority to manipulate users.

Common patterns include:
- Fake login pages mimicking banks or services
- Spoofed sender domains (e.g., support@paypa1.com)
- Requests for passwords, OTPs, or confidential data
- Malicious links disguised as legitimate URLs
- Attachments containing malware or macros
- Messages claiming account suspension or security alerts

Attackers often use social engineering tactics such as urgency ("act now"), fear ("account compromised"), or reward ("you won a prize").
